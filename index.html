When Is The Best Time To Visit Antarctica

The best time to visit Antarctica is during its summer months, from October to March when its temperature is at its mildest, the ice has melted enough for easy access to landing sites, and longer daytime hours.

How Much Does A Trip To Antarctica Cost?

A trip like this varies based on many different factors, including the itinerary, duration, level of comfort, and activities offered. Generally, a trip can cost anywhere from $5,000 to $25,000.

Crossing The Drake Passage

The Drake Passage is the body of water between South America's Cape Horn and the South Shetland Islands of Antartica. It is known for its unpredictable weather and rough seas, waves can reach heights of 30 feet or more.

Wildlife

  • The Emperor Penguin

    • The Emperor Penguin is the largest of all penguin species, they can be up to 130cm tall, and on average weighs 23kg as an adult.

  • Krill

    • The producer in Antarctica are tiny organisms, known as phytoplankton. These organisms get their energy from the sunlight. Krill is then the main consumer of the phytoplankton, which is eaten by many other organisms such as penguins, birds, or elephant seals. They are only two inches long.

  • Orca Whales

    • At the top of the food chain, there are Orcas, often known as Killer Whales. These are the biggest carnivores on Earth, reaching almost 10m in length. They can be found swimming all around the Earthâ€™s oceans, but in particular in the Southern Ocean around Antarctica.

  • Seals

    • There are many different seals in Antarctica. They have thick beautiful fur coats and blubber, making them supremely adapted to the cold. There are six species in Antarctica: Antarctic Fur Seals, Leopard Seals, Ross Seals, Southern Elephant seals, Crabeater Seals and Weddell Seals.
